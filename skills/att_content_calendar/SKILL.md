# Content Calendar ## Purpose - Plan a content system that compounds attention instead of improvising post by post. - Ad-hoc content is random. A content calendar is a system: themes that build on each other, formats matched to capacity, and cadences that create expectation. This skill designs a calendar the team can actually sustain, not an aspirational plan that dies in week 2. ## Use when - Use when the project needs recurring content around a clear audience and message strategy. - Use when content feels scattered — lots of posts, no compounding effect. - Use when launching and need a 4-8 week content runway. ## Avoid when - Do not use when the positioning and proof are still too weak to sustain real content — content without proof is just noise. - If the team can only produce 1 piece per week, don't design a daily calendar. Match the plan to the capacity. - Do not use as a substitute for `att_positioning_workshop` — strategy before tactics. ## Inputs to gather - Current content inventory: what exists, what performed, what flopped. - Audience and themes: who are you talking to, what 3-5 themes do they care about? - Team capacity: how many pieces per week can you realistically produce? - Upcoming moments: launches, features, events, seasonality, proof milestones. - Channel mix: where does the audience spend time? What formats work there? ## Operating rules - **Build around repeatable themes, not random ideas.** 3-5 themes that compound over time beat 20 one-off topics. Themes should map to your positioning pillars and the audience's persistent questions. - **Match format and cadence to available proof and production capacity.** If you can produce 2 pieces per week, plan for 2. If your proof is thin, lean educational and founder-story until proof catches up. Over-planning the calendar and under-delivering the content is the most common failure mode. - **Each piece should support trust, reach, or conversion.** Trust: proof, stories, transparency. Reach: hooks, trends, controversy. Conversion: CTA-heavy, offer-driven. Know which job each piece does. - **Design for repurposing, not original creation every time.** One deep piece (long-form post, case study, video) should spin off 3-5 derivatives (threads, quotes, short videos, social cards). Build once, distribute many. - **Leave room for reactive content.** A rigid calendar with no slack can't capitalize on trends, news, or competitor moves. Reserve 20-30% capacity for opportunistic content. ## OpenClaw tool pattern - Read existing content and analytics before planning — what already works? What's dead weight? - Use `att_content_repurposing` to plan derivatives alongside original content. - Align with `att_content_calendar` timing for launches and proof milestones from `att_launch_plan`. ## Expanded workflow 1. **Define the audience, themes, and content goals.** 3-5 themes. For each: what question does it answer, what proof supports it, what CTA does it drive toward? 2. **Choose formats and cadence that the team can sustain.** Be honest about capacity. Formula: planned output = actual capacity × 0.7 (buffer for reactive content and misses). 3. **Map the calendar to launches, stories, and proof moments.** Anchor the calendar around known events: product launches, feature drops, customer milestones, industry events. Fill the gaps with themed evergreen content. 4. **Design the repurposing system.** For each deep piece, list 3-5 derivative formats. Assign the derivative creation to the same workflow, not a separate one. 5. **Build the 4-8 week calendar.** Week-by-week: themes, formats, channels, and the CTA each piece drives toward. Include slack weeks for reactive content. 6. **Set the review rhythm.** Monthly: what performed? What flopped? What proof is newly available? Adjust the next month's plan based on data, not vibes. ## Output contract - 3-5 themes with proof and CTA per theme - Format mix: deep pieces + derivatives per week - Sustainable cadence: pieces per week, matched to team capacity - 4-8 week calendar: themes, formats, channels, CTAs per week - Repurposing plan: derivative formats per deep piece - Review rhythm: monthly performance review and plan adjustment ## Failure modes to avoid - **Over-planning, under-producing.** A beautiful 12-week calendar that requires 5 pieces/week when the team can make 2. Plan to 70% of capacity. - **No repurposing.** Every piece is built from scratch. Burnout by week 3. Build one deep, spin off many. - **All evergreen, no moments.** Content that ignores launches, news, and trends misses the attention spikes that drive growth. Anchor around moments, fill with evergreen. - **No review loop.** A calendar that never gets adjusted based on performance is a fossil by week 4. - **Ignoring what already works.** If long-form threads drive 80% of your engagement, don't pivot to short videos because they're trendy. Double down on what works. ## Handoff cues - State the themes, cadence, and capacity — another operator needs to know what's sustainable. - Name the first 2 weeks of content so execution can start immediately. - Flag any themes that lack sufficient proof and need `att_proof_mining` before content can be credible. ## Example invocation - Slash: `/att_content_calendar <task>` - Example: "I can write 2 posts per week — design a content calendar that compounds instead of random posting." - Often paired with: `att_content_repurposing`, `att_message_house`, `att_launch_plan`, `att_proof_mining` ## Quality bar - A strong calendar survives real-world bandwidth. If you can't sustain it for 8 weeks, it's too ambitious. - Every piece should have a clear job: trust, reach, or conversion. - At least 30% of content should be derivatives of deeper pieces. - The review rhythm must be built in, not aspirational. ## Related workflows - **Content system build:** `att_message_house` (themes) → `att_content_calendar` (plan) → `att_content_repurposing` (derivatives) → execute → monthly review - **Launch content runway:** `att_launch_plan` → `att_content_calendar` (8-week runway) → `att_thread_writer` / `att_short_video_script` (produce) → `att_content_repurposing` (distribute) - **Proof-driven content:** `att_proof_mining` (find proof) → `att_content_calendar` (schedule proof content) → `att_case_study_builder` (deep piece) → `att_content_repurposing` (spin off)
